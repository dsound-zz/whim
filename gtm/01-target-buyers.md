# Whim — Target Buyer List (Demand Validation Round 1)

**Goal of this list:** Find 5–8 people to get on a call this week and test whether anyone will *pay* for the NYC event feed Whim already has. This is not a sales list yet — it's a learning list. Prioritize people who (a) feel the pain acutely, (b) are small enough that a founder can reach them directly, and (c) plausibly have budget.

**The filter that matters most:** *Who is currently doing event data badly and hates it?* That pain is your wedge. Big companies build in-house; tiny ones won't pay. Aim for the middle: a real product, a small team, a clear "things to do nearby" need.

---

## Tier 1 — Start here (highest pain × most reachable)

### A. Proptech / resident-experience apps
These apps sell "neighborhood vibrancy / amenities / things to do near your building" to landlords and residents. Event data is a *feature they want but don't want to build.* This is the cleanest fit for a white-label "what's near you" feed.

- **Cobu** (resident community / events for apartment buildings)
- **Venn** (neighborhood / resident experience)
- **Amenify** (resident amenities + services marketplace)
- **ButterflyMX / Latch ecosystem** (proptech amenity surfaces)
- Boutique resident-app builders & multifamily "tenant experience" platforms

**Why they hurt:** their value prop is literally "your neighborhood is alive" — but they have no live events feed and no appetite to maintain scrapers. **Who to reach:** Head of Product / founder. **Hook:** "You promise residents a vibrant neighborhood; we hand you the live events layer as one API or an embeddable widget."

### B. Boutique hotel & concierge tech
Hotels and digital-concierge tools need "what's on tonight near the property" for guests. Luxury concierge especially.

- **ALICE, Duve, Mews** (hotel guest-experience / concierge platforms)
- Independent NYC boutique hotels with a guest app
- AI-concierge / travel-assistant startups (small ones, not OpenAI-scale)

**Why they hurt:** concierge content is manually curated and stale within a day. **Hook:** "Replace your manually-maintained 'things to do' page with a live, geofenced feed."

### C. NYC-local media & newsletters
They publish "things to do this weekend" constantly and curate it by hand.

- **Hell Gate** (NYC indie news co-op)
- **The Skint**, **Brokelyn**, **Secret NYC** (free/cheap NYC events newsletters)
- **amNewYork**, **Patch** (local news, events verticals)
- **Gothamist / WNYC** (events listings)

**Why they hurt:** an editor spends hours/week assembling listings by hand. **Hook:** "Feed your 'this weekend' roundup automatically; your editor curates instead of copy-pastes." Note: media budgets are thin — these are great *pain* interviews even if they won't pay much. Use them to learn, anchor price elsewhere.

---

## Tier 2 — Real fit, slightly harder to reach

### D. City-guide / discovery apps
- **The Infatuation / Resy** (dining discovery — adjacent, may want events)
- **Time Out** (both a potential customer *and* a partial competitor — handle carefully; good for learning what "good event data" means to a pro)
- **Thrillist**, **Citizine**-style local guides

### E. Real estate / relocation
- **StreetEasy, Compass** (neighborhood "lifestyle" content on listings)
- Relocation-services startups (helping people who just moved find local life)

**Hook:** "Make a listing's neighborhood feel alive — show what's happening within X blocks this week."

---

## Tier 3 — Interesting, but deprioritize for round 1
- Dating apps (event-based date ideas) — niche, long sales cycle.
- Transit/maps apps (Transit, Citymapper) — big, slow, build-in-house risk.
- Tourism boards (NYC Tourism + Conventions) — bureaucratic, slow money.

---

## How to build the actual call list (do this part yourself)
For each Tier 1 target:
1. Find the **founder or Head of Product** on LinkedIn.
2. Note one specific thing about their product that proves the pain (e.g., a stale "events" tab, a hand-curated "this weekend" page).
3. Add them to the tracker (`04-validation-tracker.md`).

**Target: 15 outreaches → 5–8 calls.** That's the whole experiment. Don't build anything until those calls happen.
